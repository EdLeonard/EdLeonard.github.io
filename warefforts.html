Overview

Staff and students, current and old, each year gather on the 25th of may to honour the old boys and staff that lost their lives in the Gallipoli campaign 1915. In turn those who fought in multiple different conflicts adorn the wall of the main block in honour of their sacrifice.

losses

By the end of world war one 786 boys high students students had enlisted in the war and over 20% of those students died. Furthermore in WW II 1600 boys served and more than 12% died. "One of the duties of a great school is to teach true values. Worthy examples have been given by many of those who were once boys at this school" - Howard Kippenberger, New Zealand Soldier and former CBHS old boy.
