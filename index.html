1881

CBHS established

Christchurch boys high is established in the arts center with 82 boys and 7 'masters'. School sports is done at lancaster park. The prime purpose of boys high was to prepare boys in an academical manner for the newly formed Canterbury College(now the University of Canterbury).

1883

First monitors appointed

CBHS' first monitors were appointed in 1883 sortly after the creation of the school, these monitors were made to give out fines and make sure the boys behaved.

1912

Old Boys Association Formed

The old boys association was formed on the 19th of July, 1912. The association was created to allow old boys of the school to stay in touch with their proud history and stay in contact with friends and other boys high achievements long after they had left the school.

The association was created to allow old boys of the school to stay in touch with their proud history and stay in contact with friends and other boys high achievements long after they had left the school. The old boys association has also created the Altiora Peto Medal which is awarded to an old boy who best reflects the school motto "I seek higher things" and actions are inspiration for the current boys to do better.

1916

First ANZAC service held

An ANZAC service is held in the original 'big room' to commemorate the school's losses of World War One. In turn it was said the headmaster of the time Mr C.E Bevan-Brown would read out the names of Old Boys who had died fighting in World War One, and even become very emotionally moved talking about these losses.

1917

Adams house is opened

Adams house is opened, tasked to allow talented boys from out of school zoning to attend Christchurch Boys High.

1926

Boys High is moved to riccarton

Boy's High is moved from the city center as Christchurch was becoming to big a town for a high school. The new land is donated by the Deans family and is 28 acres of space.

1939

Old Boys serve in WWII

1600 Old Boys serve in WWII as well as 11 staff. 200 of these Old Boys lose their lives.

1997

The new hall is opened

The new school hall is opened to the public. It is far more modern then the one before it and will be used for another 18 years before the current hall is built.

2005

Prince William visits the school

Prince William visits CBHS constantly under 3 levels of massive secruity. He speaks at an assembly for the boys.
